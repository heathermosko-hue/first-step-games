📌 3 Tips to Kickstart Your Child's Reading

1
Start with letter sounds, not letter names. Knowing that "B says buh" is more useful for reading than knowing its name. Teach sounds first — names will follow naturally.
2
Keep sessions short and playful. 5–10 minutes of focused, fun practice beats 30 minutes of drilling. Stop while they're still enjoying it so they always want more.
3
Read aloud together every day. Even after your child starts reading on their own, shared reading builds vocabulary, comprehension, and a love of books that lasts a lifetime.
