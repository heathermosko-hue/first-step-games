✨ Why First Step Reading Works

First Step Reading makes learning to read simple, fun, and effective.

Children start by learning letter sounds. Once they know the sounds, they learn how to blend them together to read words like at, cat, and hat. They also learn word families, which helps them quickly read many new words using patterns they already know.

At the same time, children learn important sight words (high-frequency words) such as the, is, and, and you. These are common words that appear often in books and everyday reading.

As children grow, they move on to more advanced reading skills, including:

  • Digraphs (sh, ch, th, wh)
  • Consonant blends (bl, st, tr, cl)
  • Long vowel sounds
  • Silent letters
  • Common spelling patterns
  • Tricky and irregular words

By combining phonics, sight words, books, videos, and games, children build strong reading skills step by step.

The result is a confident reader who can sound out new words, recognize common words instantly, and enjoy reading independently.
